Public Safety Notice (04/20/99)

Several deaths have been reported near the East Ridge Development construction zone. No confirmed connection to site operations. Authorities urge caution. Construction continues with increased vigilance. Please report any suspicious activity.

Tip Line: REDACTED
Case #1999-01 (Found April 15)
Lawson, Brian

Location: Inside collapsed section of abandoned pipe near construction perimeter.
Notes: Found deceased with multiple abrasions and deep wrist lacerations. Evidence indicates the person was trapped and tried to escape. Toxicology pending. Dirt patterns suggest victim crawled a long distance before collapse.

Status: Under investigation
Case #1999-02 (Found April 17)
Becker, Jeffery

Location: Seated against chain-link fence along site perimeter road
Notes: Found deceased with disheveled clothing. Pager found with an unsent draft message saying: “Someone snooping around. Have you noticed anyone on site?” No footprints or signs of struggle nearby. Possible confusion or panic prior to death.

Status: Unresolved
Case #1999-03 (Found April 19)
Rogers, Mary

Location: Waterlogged underbrush near creek adjacent to construction zone
Notes: Found deceased with body partially submerged showing bruises and lacerations on legs and ankles. Scene suggests accidental fall or disorientation. Female victim has no known affiliations with East Ridge Development.

Status: Under Investigation
Case #1999-04 (Found April 22)
Caldwell, Todd

Location: Maintenance tunnel beneath construction site
Notes: Found after prolonged search. Helmet removed, gloves torn and scattered. Deep scratch marks on tunnel walls suggest panic or struggle. Last radio transmission includes heavy static and labored breathing. Victim likely experienced severe distress prior to collapse.

Status: Under investigation
Case #1999-05 (Found April 25)
Martinez, Eric

Location: Adjacent utility substation, ~0.5 miles from main site
Notes: Found deceased near patrol vehicle. Last radio transmission cut off abruptly after repeated calls for help. Minor injuries likely sustained after moving through foliage. Radio damaged but functional.

Status: Under investigation

Worker Incident Report (05/12/99)

One worker has been found semi-conscious near the maintenance tunnel edge. Individual was rushed to St. Amelia Hospital in critical condition. After several days, individual regained enough lucidity to recall the experience: Reported being followed by a tall shadowy figure inside the tunnels with features matching descriptions previously retrieved. Described confusion, dizziness, and a subtle foul odor in days leading to incident. Symptoms included severe disorientation and respiratory distress before losing consciousness. Medical evaluation ongoing; toxicology pending.

Status: Critical, surviving witness
IMPORTANT - FINAL STATEMENT
05/19/99
Following comprehensive testing by Hazmat and state environmental authorities, all fatalities previously investigated as homicides have been reclassified as accidental toxic exposures.

A previously unaccounted containment line was found ruptured beneath the Eastview Development construction site. The rupture had released long‑sealed industrial gases into adjacent drainage and utility structures. These gases are now known to cause progressive disorientation, hallucinations, paranoia, and fatal respiratory failure after prolonged exposure.

All prior reports of a figure are consistent with gas‑induced sensory disturbances and paranoia rather than the presence of a suspect. No evidence of foul play has been confirmed. However, authorities continue to search for evidence of conspiracy.

Construction remains suspended while remediation is underway. Environmental and legal teams are now coordinating efforts to assess potential negligence in the land’s transfer to Eastview Development. Preliminary findings indicate discrepancies in historical site records and safety certifications provided during the sale.

Authorities thank the community for cooperation.
